Pastel de nata

Description

Pastel de nata is a Portuguese egg tart pastry that has a creamy custard filling and a flaky crust, often enjoyed with a sprinkle of cinnamon or powdered sugar.

It is a beloved treat in Portugal and has gained popularity worldwide, known for its rich flavor and delightful texture.

Ingredients

  • 1 sheet of puff pastry (store-bought or homemade)
  • 250 ml (1 cup) whole milk
  • g (2 tbsp) all-purpose flour
  • 50 g (1¼ cups) granulated sugar
  • 125 ml (½ cup) water
  • 6 large egg yolks
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 strip of lemon peel (just the yellow part)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 250°C (480°F).
  2. In a saucepan, combine sugar, water, cinnamon stick, and lemon peel. Bring to a boil and cook until the sugar dissolves. Remove from heat and discard cinnamon and lemon peel.
  3. In another saucepan, whisk flour into milk and cook over medium heat until thickened and smooth.
  4. Slowly pour the sugar syrup into the milk-flour mixture, whisking constantly. Let cool slightly.
  5. Whisk egg yolks into the cooled mixture until fully combined and smooth.
  6. Roll out the puff pastry and cut into circles to fit a muffin tin. Press pastry into the tin, making sure it comes up the sides.
  7. Fill each pastry shell with custard mixture, about ¾ full.
  8. Bake for 15–20 minutes until the tops are caramelized and the pastry is golden.
  9. Let cool slightly before serving. Optionally, sprinkle with cinnamon or powdered sugar.
